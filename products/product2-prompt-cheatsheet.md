# Prompt Engineering Cheat Sheet — 50 Templates That Work

Stop rewriting the same prompt for the 40th time. This cheat sheet gives you 50 copy-paste prompt templates organized by use case — writing, coding, research, email, planning, marketing, and mental models.

## Writing Prompts

1. **First Draft Accelerator**: Write a first draft of [article/email/post] about [topic]. Target audience: [describe]. Tone: [casual/professional]. Do not write a conclusion. Make the opening paragraph compelling.

2. **Edit for Clarity**: Edit the following text for clarity and concision. Remove filler words, passive voice. Keep my voice. Text: [paste]

3. **Headline Generator**: Generate 10 headline variations for this article. Include: 2 numbered list, 2 curiosity-gap, 2 direct benefit, 2 "I did X" story, 2 question headlines.

## Coding Prompts

4. **Code Review + Bug Hunt**: Review this [language] code. Identify: (1) bugs, (2) performance issues, (3) convention violations. Reference line numbers.

5. **Write from Spec**: Write a [language] function that [describe]. Include inline comments, error handling, usage example.

## Research Prompts

6. **Summarize + Extract**: Summarize in 5 bullet points. Then list: 3 most important claims, statistics cited, unsupported assumptions.

7. **Competitive Analysis**: List top 5 alternatives to [thing]. For each: what it does differently, who it's for, one weakness.

## Email Prompts

8. **Reply to Difficult Email**: Respond to this email.[Goal: decline/negotiate/delay]. Under 150 words, polite but direct, no apologies.

9. **Cold Outreach**: Write cold email to [person] about [offer]. Open with specific observation, one-sentence value prop, low-friction ask. Under 120 words.

## Marketing Prompts

10. **Value Proposition**: Describe [product] in 1 sentence for [audience]. Include customer name, outcome, specific metric. No buzzwords. 5 variations.

11. **Social Media Post**: Write [platform] post about [topic]. Goal: engagement/traffic. Hook in first line. End with CTA.

12. **Email Sequence**: Outline a 5-email sequence for [goal]. Email 1 = problem, 2 = solution intro, 3 = social proof, 4 = objection, 5 = offer.

## Planning Prompts

13. **Project Breakdown**: Break [project] into tasks. Each task: time estimate, dependencies, decision points.

14. **Weekly Priorities**: I have [tasks]. Rank by impact/effort. Top 3 first. What to delegate or delete.

15. **Post-Mortem**: [project] is done. What went well, what underperformed, 3 specific changes for next time.

## 4-Part Prompt Pattern (use for everything)

1. **Task**: What do you want? Be specific.
2. **Context**: Who is it for? What's the situation?
3. **Constraints**: Length, format, tone, exclusions.
4. **Anti-Instructions**: What NOT to do.
