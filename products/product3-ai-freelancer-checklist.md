# The AI Freelancer Startup Checklist

**Launch an AI-powered freelancing business in 7 days. One page. Every box matters.**

Most "start freelancing" advice is a 20-hour course you'll never finish. This is the opposite: a checklist that fits on a single screen and gets you from zero to "accepting clients" in a week.

The goal is not to build the perfect business. The goal is to have a real offer, a real profile, and a real inbox where real prospects can reach you — before the weekend.

Work through it in order. Check boxes as you go. If you're stuck on a box for more than 30 minutes, move on and come back.

---

## Day 1 — Pick Your Wedge (2 hours)

- [ ] **Pick ONE AI service you will sell.** Not three, not a menu — one. Examples: AI content editing, ChatGPT workflow setup, prompt engineering for founders, AI-assisted research, AI-powered SEO content, cold-email copywriting with AI, resume rewriting with AI, customer support knowledge base building.
- [ ] **Pick ONE target customer.** Write a 1-sentence description: *"[Role] at [company type] who struggles with [problem]."* Example: *"Solo SaaS founders who need to ship content but can't afford a full-time writer."*
- [ ] **Write your value proposition in one sentence.** Format: *"I help [customer] achieve [outcome] using [service] — in [timeframe] for [price range]."*
- [ ] **Set your starting price.** Floor: $300 for a finished deliverable, $500 for a mini project, $1,500 for a small engagement. Do not charge hourly on day one.
- [ ] **Commit to a 90-day window.** Write it down: *"From [today's date] to [90 days from now], I will try this wedge full-heartedly before changing it."*

---

## Day 2 — Ship the Minimum Viable Offer (3 hours)

- [ ] **Define your offer in 3 bullet points.** What they get. When they get it. How much it costs. Nothing else.
- [ ] **Build ONE proof artifact.** Do the service for free, once, on a sample project. Save it. This becomes your case study.
- [ ] **Write a 1-page offer doc.** Sections: Problem, Solution, Deliverables, Timeline, Price, Process. Keep it under 500 words.
- [ ] **Create a simple scheduling link.** Cal.com or Calendly free tier. Name it something clear like "AI [Service] Discovery Call — 20 min."
- [ ] **Decide your "no" list.** Three things you will not do (examples: no unpaid trial projects, no per-hour billing, no scope beyond the offer doc without a change order).

---

## Day 3 — Build Your Storefront (3 hours)

- [ ] **Update your LinkedIn headline.** Format: *"Helping [customer] [outcome] with AI | [Service] | Free discovery call →"*
- [ ] **Rewrite your LinkedIn About section.** 3 paragraphs: who you help, the problem you solve, how to work with you. Include the scheduling link.
- [ ] **Pick ONE more channel** (Twitter/X, Indie Hackers, a Slack community, a subreddit — only one). Update the bio/profile with the same positioning.
- [ ] **Post one intro post** on your chosen channel. Format: *"I just started offering [service] for [customer]. Here's why [pain point] is a real problem and here's my approach. DM or book a call: [link]."*
- [ ] **Screenshot your proof artifact** and add it to the post as proof.

---

## Day 4 — Prospecting Infrastructure (2 hours)

- [ ] **Build a prospect list of 50 names.** LinkedIn Sales Navigator trial, Apollo free tier, or manual search. Column headers: Name, Role, Company, Why they fit, Email if public.
- [ ] **Write 3 cold outreach templates.** Keep each under 150 words. Hooks: (1) specific observation, (2) relevant outcome, (3) low-friction ask.
- [ ] **Set up a simple tracking sheet.** Columns: Prospect, Date sent, Opened?, Replied?, Call booked?, Outcome.
- [ ] **Batch-block a daily 30-min "sending" slot** on your calendar. Same time, every day. This is the habit that compounds.

---

## Day 5 — First Outreach Batch (2 hours)

- [ ] **Send 5 personalized cold emails** to the warmest prospects on your list. Customize the opening line for each — no mass sends.
- [ ] **Send 3 LinkedIn connection requests** to prospects. Include a 1-sentence note mentioning why you're reaching out. Do not pitch in the connect note.
- [ ] **Post one piece of content** related to your service. Example: a mini case study from your proof artifact, or a tip that demonstrates expertise.
- [ ] **Reply to any DMs, comments, or emails** within 2 hours. Speed is a differentiator.
- [ ] **Log every outbound action** in your tracking sheet. No exceptions.

---

## Day 6 — Close the Loop (2 hours)

- [ ] **Follow up with anyone who hasn't replied.** One-sentence bump, 3 days after initial send. Example: *"Hi [Name], just wanted to make sure this didn't get buried — any thoughts?"*
- [ ] **Prepare your discovery call framework.** 20-min structure: 2 min rapport, 10 min discovery questions, 5 min offer walk-through, 3 min next steps.
- [ ] **Write 5 discovery questions.** Examples: *"What would success look like in 90 days?"*, *"What have you tried before?"*, *"What's the cost of not solving this?"*
- [ ] **Draft a simple proposal template.** One page. Sections: Understanding, Scope, Timeline, Investment, Payment terms, Next step.
- [ ] **Create an invoice template.** Use Stripe, Wave, or even a Google Doc. Due on receipt, or 50% up front for projects over $1,500.

---

## Day 7 — Systems for Week 2 (2 hours)

- [ ] **Review Week 1 numbers.** Prospects contacted, replies received, calls booked. Write the ratios down.
- [ ] **Identify your bottleneck.** Not enough prospects? Not enough replies? Not enough calls booking? Only one will be true.
- [ ] **Commit to fixing ONE thing in Week 2.** Not all three. Example: *"Week 2 I will send 10 outbound per day instead of 5."*
- [ ] **Write 2 content pieces** about your niche. Schedule one for Monday, one for Thursday.
- [ ] **Book a "business review" meeting with yourself** for next Sunday. 30 min. Review numbers, adjust one thing.

---

## The Meta-Rules

These override any single checkbox:

- **Ship rough things fast.** A bad v1 that exists beats a perfect v1 that doesn't.
- **Talk to prospects, don't build in secret.** Every week without a customer conversation is a wasted week.
- **Charge real money from day one.** Free work is optional and rare. Paid work builds the business.
- **One service, one customer, for 90 days.** Switching wedges is the #1 killer of new freelancers.
- **AI is the tool, not the product.** You are selling an outcome. AI is how you deliver it faster or cheaper. Nobody cares which model you use.

---

## What success looks like at Day 30

- 100+ prospects contacted
- 10+ calls booked
- 2–3 paying clients closed
- $1,500–$5,000 in first-month revenue
- One signed testimonial or case study
- A clear #1 bottleneck for month 2

If you hit these numbers, double down. If you miss them badly, the wedge is wrong — not you. Pick a different customer or service and run the same checklist again.

---

## What NOT to do in Week 1

These are the mistakes that kill new freelancers. Do not do these:

- **Don't build a website before you have a client.** A LinkedIn profile and a one-page offer doc is enough.
- **Don't make a logo.** You will change it. It does not matter.
- **Don't register an LLC in week one.** Invoice as a sole proprietor. Set up an LLC after your third paid client.
- **Don't take calls without qualification.** "Coffee chats" with people who are not prospects are how weeks disappear.
- **Don't discount on your first call.** If they push back on price, ask *"what would feel fair to you?"* and see what they say.
- **Don't quit your job yet.** Run this on the side until you have 3 months of expenses in freelance revenue saved.

---

## The 7-Day Reality Check

You won't complete every box perfectly. That's fine. The point of a checklist is not to execute it like a robot. It's to eliminate decision fatigue — so you spend your energy on the work, not on figuring out what the work is.

Do Day 1 today. The rest will fall into place.
